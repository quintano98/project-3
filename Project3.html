First Aid Kit Essential Must Have

A disaster can result in numerous injuries, to yourself or to those around you, including family, coworkers, and neighbors. Having an emergency first aid kit with the right equipment is essential for providing care to the injured in the hours and days to follow.

ABSORBENT COMPRESS DRESSINGS

Use for the management of moderate to heavily exudating wounds.

ADHESIVE PAD

Provides cushioned protection to minor burns, scrapes, cuts and abrasions; Strong adhesive stays on during water activities.

ADHESIVE CLOTH TAPE

Used to attach bandages, gauze, and other dressings to the skin around wounds.

ANTIBIOTIC OINTMENT

Helps to prevent and treat most minor skin infections that come from small cuts, scratches, scrapes, or minor burns

ANTISEPTIC WIPE PACKET

Used to cleanse a cut prior to administering other applications such as an ointment or bandage.

INSTANT COLD PACK

Used to reduce swelling and relieve discomfort from insect bites to sport injuries.

EMERGENCY BLANKET

Vital when facing chaos and life-threatening situations..

BREATHING BARRIER

Protect responders from potential exposure to germs and infectious diseases during resuscitation.

THERMOMETER

Use a Thermometer to check for fever and body themperature

NONLATEX GLOVES

Ideal choice for anyone who has to handle potentially hazardous and corrosive chemicals

HYDROCORTISONE OINTMENT

Used to help relieve redness, itching, swelling, or other discomfort caused by skin conditions.

GAUZE ROLL

Used to hold a dressing in place on a wound, maintain pressure, to control bleeding, support an injured limb or joint, and apply pressure to a limb..

TWEEZERS

Medical tweezers are an auxiliary tool that is used for various manipulations.

ASPIRINS

Used to reduce fever and to relieve mild to moderate pain from headaches, menstrual periods, arthritis, toothaches, and muscle aches.

TRIANGULAR BANDAGES

Can be used as large dressings, as slings to support a limb, or to secure a dressing in place.
